Case studies.

01

0 → 500,000 MAU as the only instructional designer

Context

Tixu.ai — an AI education product for the US/EU markets. All learning content was one person's responsibility: mine.

What I did

Designed 85+ courses and 1,100+ lessons in English. Worked with the CPO and two PMs on product hypotheses and metrics. Built the production system so one designer could serve a product of half a million users.

Result

From zero to 500,000 MAU in a year. Course CSI — 80%+.

02

×26: an AI pipeline for course production and localization

Context

The product was growing faster than a human could write lessons. Production had to scale without losing quality — and without growing the team.

What I did

Broke course development into reproducible LLM steps with generator, critic and editor roles, and built-in quality gates. Automated localization from English into 4 languages.

Result

Shipping one course became 26× faster.

03

04

05

Context

Users were churning in the first days. The default answer is "make more content". I went to the data instead.

Focused on the first-time user experience: designed experiments with the PMs, ran them, and reworked the learning logic of the first lessons.

Result

Day-7 retention tripled.

06

Eduson, lead of the instructional design team. Led two teams in parallel and owned the project triangle: budget, deadlines, resources.

Built processes and quality standards, ran the department's internship, hired 4 people full-time, grew one specialist into a team lead. Refactored three products.

07

Russia's #1 counselor school: a methodology that scales through people

Context

Before EdTech I taught psychology and pedagogy at a university — and was named teacher of the year. In parallel, I coached teachers at a counselor school.

Ran workshops for teachers before and during the course — from behavioral psychology to public speaking. Facilitated classes and gave feedback after every lecture.
